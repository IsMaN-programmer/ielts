News

As of January 2025, here are the latest updates regarding the IELTS (International English Language Testing System):

Increased Availability of Computer-Based IELTS: The computer-delivered IELTS test has continued to expand in terms of availability and locations. Candidates now have more opportunities to take the computer-based test, which offers quicker results (typically in 3-5 days) compared to the traditional paper-based test. The computer-based version is now available in a wider range of countries.

IELTS Indicator for Remote Areas: Due to ongoing global uncertainties, IELTS Indicator was launched during the COVID-19 pandemic as a way for candidates to take the test remotely. While this was initially a temporary solution, it continues to be an option for students in certain regions, particularly in areas where in-person tests might not be accessible.

Changes to Speaking Test: The IELTS Speaking test has seen some updates in terms of how feedback is provided. Candidates are now given more comprehensive insights into their performance after the test, which allows them to better understand their strengths and areas for improvement.

IELTS and Visa Requirements: There have been updates regarding the use of IELTS scores for visa applications in countries like Australia, Canada, the UK, and others. These countries may adjust their minimum score requirements depending on policy changes or shifts in immigration rules. It's important for test-takers to stay informed on these evolving requirements.
